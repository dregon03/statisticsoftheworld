# BRICS vs G7: An Economic Comparison Using Real Data (2026 Edition)

The BRICS narrative has been everywhere lately — but how does the economic reality actually compare to the G7? I pulled the latest IMF and World Bank data to run through the numbers.

## Quick framing

**G7**: US, Germany, Japan, UK, France, Italy, Canada
**BRICS+**: Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa — plus the newer additions (UAE, Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran)

I'm using nominal GDP, GDP per capita (PPP-adjusted), and a few quality-of-life indicators to get a complete picture.

---

## 1. Total GDP: BRICS has overtaken G7 on a PPP basis

By **nominal GDP** (current USD), the G7 still leads comfortably:
- G7 combined: ~$48 trillion
- BRICS original 5: ~$29 trillion

But by **PPP-adjusted GDP** — which accounts for the fact that a dollar goes a lot further in India than in Germany — the BRICS5 surpassed the G7 around 2021 and the gap has widened since.

The IMF now projects BRICS+ economies to account for over 35% of global GDP (PPP) by 2030, up from roughly 28% a decade ago.

*[Data: IMF World Economic Outlook, April 2026, via statisticsoftheworld.com/compare]*

---

## 2. GDP per capita: the G7 dominance is staggering

This is where the BRICS story gets complicated. The GDP totals are large because the populations are enormous — not because citizens are wealthy.

GDP per capita (PPP, 2026 estimates):
- **US**: ~$85,000
- **Germany**: ~$68,000
- **UK**: ~$57,000
- **China**: ~$24,000
- **Brazil**: ~$19,000
- **South Africa**: ~$15,000
- **India**: ~$10,000

China's rise here is real and notable — it's roughly quadrupled per-capita income in 20 years. But there's still a very long way to go to reach G7 levels.

---

## 3. Inflation: the divergence in 2022-2024 was significant

Post-COVID inflation hit both blocs hard, but the shapes were different:
- G7 countries saw inflation peak around 8-10% in 2022, then come down sharply as central banks raised rates aggressively.
- Several BRICS members (Brazil, Russia after sanctions) had already been dealing with structural inflation pressures that preceded the pandemic.
- India managed the transition relatively well — inflation stayed under 7% through most of the period.
- China was the outlier in the other direction, with deflationary pressure more recently.

---

## 4. Debt levels: a surprisingly mixed picture

The common assumption is that G7 countries are heavily indebted while BRICS are more conservative. Reality is messier:

- **Japan**: 263% debt-to-GDP (highest in the developed world)
- **Italy**: ~145%
- **China**: ~85% official, but local government debt pushes the real figure much higher
- **India**: ~85% and rising
- **Brazil**: ~90% and has had recurring fiscal crises
- **Russia**: ~20% (sanctions isolation has forced a kind of austerity)

---

## 5. What the data can't tell you

Numbers are only part of the picture. G7 economies still dominate in:
- Technology and innovation (R&D spending, patent filings)
- Financial system depth (capital markets, reserve currency)
- Institutional quality (rule of law, property rights — which affects long-run growth)

BRICS advantages are mostly demographic (younger populations in India, Africa) and resource-based.
