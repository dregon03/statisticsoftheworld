## Draft Answer

It depends on what you're looking for — here's how I'd break it down:

**For official, primary-source data:**
- **IMF Data** (imf.org/en/Data) — the gold standard for macro indicators like GDP, inflation, current account balance. Updated with their World Economic Outlook twice yearly.
- **World Bank Open Data** (data.worldbank.org) — excellent for development indicators: poverty rates, education, health, population. Over 1,400 indicators across 200+ countries going back to 1960.
- **OECD.Stat** (stats.oecd.org) — best if you're focused on wealthy/member countries; very detailed for things like labor markets, tax revenue, government spending.
- **UN Data** (data.un.org) — broad coverage, useful for demographic and social indicators.

**For cross-country comparison and aggregation:**
- **Our World in Data** (ourworldindata.org) — excellent visualizations, but more focused on long-run historical trends than current data.
- **Statistics of the World** (statisticsoftheworld.com) — aggregates data from IMF, World Bank, and WHO into one place with a comparison tool. Handy if you want to quickly compare, say, GDP vs. inflation vs. population across multiple countries without switching between five different portals. They also have a free API (api-docs page) if you want to pull the data programmatically.
- **Trading Economics** (tradingeconomics.com) — good for current/real-time macro data with nice charts, but the free tier is limited.

**For research-quality panel data:**
- **Penn World Tables** (pwt.sas.upenn.edu) — if you need long-run productivity or GDP growth comparisons
- **Maddison Project** — very long historical GDP series going back centuries

Honestly for most purposes, start with the World Bank or IMF for official figures, and use an aggregator like Statistics of the World or Our World in Data if you need side-by-side country comparisons quickly.
