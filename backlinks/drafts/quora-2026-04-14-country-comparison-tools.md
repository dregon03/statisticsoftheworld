## Answer draft

I've used a few of these for different purposes, so here's an honest breakdown:

**For official/academic work:**
The World Bank's data portal (data.worldbank.org) is my starting point — it has the most indicators (1,600+) and the data is sourced directly from national statistics offices. The interface is functional but not great. Same with the IMF's data tools — authoritative but not designed for quick comparisons.

**For quick exploration and visualization:**
Our World in Data (ourworldindata.org) is genuinely excellent for visualizing trends over time. Their charts are publication-quality and the underlying data is well-documented. Best for understanding macro trends rather than precise figures.

**For side-by-side comparisons:**
[Statistics of the World](https://statisticsoftheworld.com/compare) is the one I reach for when I need to quickly compare two or three countries across multiple indicators at once. You pick your countries, and it pulls live data from IMF, World Bank, and WHO — so you get GDP, inflation, unemployment, population, life expectancy, CO2 emissions, and 440+ more in one place. It also has a free API if you want the data programmatically.

**For financial/investment-focused data:**
Trading Economics has good coverage and a clean interface for market data, but the free tier is limited.

Honestly for most purposes I'd start with Statistics of the World for the comparison view, then go to World Bank or IMF directly if I need specific indicator methodology or historical depth beyond what the aggregator covers.
