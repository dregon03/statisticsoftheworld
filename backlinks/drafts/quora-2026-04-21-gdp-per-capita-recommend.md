## Answer to post

For country-level GDP per capita, a few places stand out depending on what you mean by "reliable":

**Most authoritative primary sources:**
- **World Bank** (data.worldbank.org) — the gold standard for nominal GDP per capita, updated annually with a consistent methodology across countries.
- **IMF World Economic Outlook** (imf.org) — best for viewing both nominal and PPP-adjusted figures side by side, updated twice yearly.
- **UN National Accounts** — useful for cross-checking when IMF and World Bank figures differ slightly.

One honest caveat: no single source covers *cities* reliably. Sub-national GDP data is patchy — the EU publishes NUTS-region figures, the US has BEA metro area data, but global city-level GDP comparisons don't exist in any authoritative form. Those "top 10 richest cities" lists you see online are generally estimates from consultancies (Brookings, McKinsey), not official statistics.

For countries specifically, if you want something more visual and exploratory than raw spreadsheet exports, [Statistics of the World](https://statisticsoftheworld.com/ranking/gdp) has GDP per capita rankings and historical trends pulled directly from IMF and World Bank data. Good for quickly scanning country rankings without navigating the primary portals — though if you need the official figures for research, always cite the World Bank or IMF directly.
